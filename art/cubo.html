Cubo

This is one of my homage projects in which I replicate work of Edward Zajec’s “Il Cubo” from 1971. Here's an articles written in Italian that helps visually explain how the images are created.

Basically there are 8 different tile types that all represent different parts of cube. The programs starts by randomly placing white tiles. The logical rules kick in to determine what all the neighboring tiles of each white square should be. Overall it was a really fun project and I always enjoy recreating the logic of those who came before me.

I do plan on revisiting this in order to create purposeful shapes such as space invaders and to add color which has been surpisingly difficult with my current implementation. All of that will be a seperate project.
